Bulawayo

Bulawayo, the City of Kings, is Zimbabwe’s cultural soul, known for its wide, jacaranda-lined streets and a pace that’s refreshingly laid-back. Its name—KoBulawayo—meaning "the place of the one to be killed," echoes its fierce royal history under King Lobengula. Beyond the vintage red-brick buildings and the world-class Natural History Museum, the city serves as the gateway to the mystical granite hills of Matobo National Park. It’s a place where history feels alive, the hospitality is famously warm, and every corner reflects a proud, regal heritage.
